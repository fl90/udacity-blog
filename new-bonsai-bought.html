repotting, styling and wiring of raw material

Blog > New Bonsai bought
Today i decided to buy raw material from an ordinary garden center and try to make a bonsai out of it.

So i hopped in my car and drove to the nearest hardware store. In germany they usually have an area for plants and flowers in there. I wandered throught their evergreens and deciduous trees and found an hinoki cypress. Luckily there were 20% off of every plant that day.
caption: this is what i ended up with

What to do to make this a beautiful bonsai?

Back home i removed the small branches that are growing directly out of the main trunk. A beautiful bonsai shows the trunk. Otherwise its only a bush.

After removing the small branches i could see the trunk and decided which thicker branches i wanna keep. The decision was made and i removed the branches that i donÂ´t need anymore. I also removed small green branches that start right were the thicker branches grew out of the main trunk.

Now we have to wire the branches. Best way to do this is to wire always two branches together. Wrap the wire at a 45 degree angle around the branch, starting at the main trunk.

How do you show more of the trunk?

Raw material is usually planted very deep in the pot. Removed the plant from the pot and scratch away the top of the soil. Maybe you'll find a larger trunk or beautiful looking surface roots.

Repot the bonsai in proper pot and this could be the your result.
